English
🎯 Strategic Vision
This project simulates a data consultancy focused on capital efficiency. The primary goal is to analyze customer lifetime value (LTV) and acquisition costs (CAC) to determine the true profitability of each marketing channel, enabling data-driven budget allocation.

⚙️ Analysis Protocol (Professional Framework)
To ensure analytical rigor, I followed a structured 10-step pipeline:

Structural Audit: Data type validation and integrity check.

Cleaning & Normalization: Handling nulls and temporal conversion.

Product Metrics: Retention and activity analysis (DAU/WAU/MAU).

Sales Analysis: Conversion rates and average ticket value.

Marketing Economics: Calculation of CAC, LTV, and ROMI by cohorts.

Business Drivers: Identifying growth levers.

Recommendations: Tactical action plan.

💡 Key Business Insights
Channel Optimization: Identified Sources 1, 2, 5, and 9 as the most profitable.

Budget Reallocation: Recommended shifting investment from Source 3 (low relative return) to sources with higher accumulated LTV.

Operational Efficiency: Optimizing average conversion time (60s) to maximize funnel performance.

🛠️ Tecnologias / Tech Stack
Language: Python 3.x

Data Processing: Pandas, NumPy

Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn

Methodology: Cohort Analysis, Unit Economics (LTV/CAC)
